The Problem I Lived

I spent years working in hospitality with an accounting background. Every single venue I worked with had the same problem: fragmented data in spreadsheets that were always out of date.

Recipe costs? In one spreadsheet. Sales data? Export from POS, paste into Excel. Inventory? Either guessing or counting everything manually. Supplier prices changing? Update 47 different cells and hope you didn't miss one.

The financial rigor was there - I knew what should be tracked. But the tools didn't exist to make it practical for busy hospitality operators.

So I built VenueIQ. The analytics platform I wish I had when I was doing this work manually.

Why This Matters

Most restaurant software is built by tech people who've never worked a shift. They add features that sound good but don't match reality.

VenueIQ is different. It's built by someone who knows both worlds - the accounting discipline and the operational chaos.

Our Philosophy

The principles that guide how we build VenueIQ

Real-Time or Nothing

Static reports are useless by the time you see them. If the data isn't live, it's not actionable.

Build Fast, Learn Fast

We ship quickly and iterate based on real feedback from real operators. No 18-month roadmaps.

Honest About Limits

We're in early access. Some things aren't built yet. We're transparent about what works and what doesn't.

Where We Are Now

Early Access, Actively Building

VenueIQ is live and functional. We have 4 integrated modules working in production. But we're still actively improving based on feedback from early adopters.

We're not pretending to be a finished product with thousands of customers. We're a founder building in public, working directly with beta partners to make this thing actually useful.
